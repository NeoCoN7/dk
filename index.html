Red Raptor Consultancy LTD

Business Manager • January 2015 - Present

I was the Business Manager at a Business and IT Consultancy, Red Raptor Consultancy. It is my job to ensure the smooth day to day operation of the company. I speak with clients, usually at a face to face meeting where I discuss their needs. Once we have nailed down what they are looking for I write a proposal showing them what we can offer them. When I am not out visiting clients, I am responsible for cultivating business relationships, compiling Tech Briefs/Specs and issuing all quotes.

When a contract is won I manage the project from start to finish. From working with the designers to sending out the final invoice I cover all bases (the perks of working in a small company!). A lot of my day is spent talking to clients, establishing their needs and managing projects. I speak to clients face to face, over the phone/skype, via Skype and through IM on Slack.

I also handle the management of our suppliers such as designers, out sourced developers etc. I ensure that they work to an agreed schedule, that the work is completed to a high standard and offer input where necessary. Recently I have taken on board the role of managing an IT apprentice. I manage them day to day while teaching them new aspects related to their chosen field.

Working at Red Raptor gave me experience in long term project manaagement and web deveopment projects.

Maiden Cleaning

Business Manager • July 2013 - December 2014

During my time at Maiden Cleaning LTD (formally AyeClean LTD) I ran the day to day operations of the company. Although I was hired as the business manager I ended up taking a lot of the admin duties too.

The bulk of time my was spent managing the team of 10 cleaners. I would ensure that they were at work, working to a high standard and were happy in their roles. When someone called in sick it was up to me to find cover to ensure the day still went smoothly. In the rare event no cover was available I would rework the diary ensuring that all clients were still covered that week.

While not dealing with staff I would speak to clients over the phone and email. I would issue invoices, resolve complaints and adjust the service based on the clients needs.

My time at Maiden Cleaning gave me invaluable experience in managign staff and conflict resolution.

Scottish Widows

Call Handler/Coach/Subject Matter Expert• May 2006 - July 2013

At Scottish Widows I started off as a Call Handler trained on dealing Existing Business queries relating to Assurance and Bonds. I was quickly upskilled to also deal with calls relating to Penisions and Annuities. At the end of 2006 I was one of the first people trained on a new Life Assurance Product, Protection For Life. By the time I left, I was the most "cross skilled" person in the call centre.

Alongside my call handling duties I was promoted first to Mentor and then to Subject Matter Expert for the Protection For Life (P4L) product. This required me to take on several new responsibilities while continuing to deal with around 50 calls per day. I was the first point of contact for the P4L product and this meant I would have to liaise with clients, team members and senior management/directors ensuring that everything was running smoothly.

I was also given extra responsibilities in the form of carrying out training for both new and exisiting staff, call scoring and involved in several projects. I successfully trained around 50 members of staff during my time at Scottish Widows, many of whom are still there and are in management positions.

Working at Scottish Widows gave me an excellent background in telephony based Customer Service and Staff Training/Coaching.
